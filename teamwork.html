Teamwork

    Teamwork & Service

    In both my academic life, and in a professional setting I have evidenced that I thrive in a teamwork-oriented environment. Being a social person, I enjoy both the casual banter and the tough conversations when it comes to big decisions; and I usually don’t say no to a good challenge.

    I have always taken on the opinion that the best way to serve your teammates is to exemplify what you would value in your teammates. One example of this is in my Deep Learning and Convolutional Neural Networks Major Project, where there was three group members, including myself. One group member was strong in Frontend Engineering, and the other group member’s strengths were not in Software Development. I took ownership of the situation and proposed that we delegate the work between us. I would focus on developing the AI model and training pipeline, one would focus on developing the frontend WebApp, and the other will focus on researching and collating resources for us to use. I took on the brunt of the work in this situation, but I was well placed for the challenge, and I wanted to ensure the project’s success by taking ownership of work that was best suited to my skills. This I think is a fine example of servant leadership – and something that I strive to achieve wherever possible.
